# Zero-Shot Voice Conversion

**Summary**: Zero-shot voice conversion converts to or from speakers unseen during conversion-model training. AutoVC supports this by using a pretrained speaker encoder to represent unseen target speakers from reference utterances.

**Sources**:
- Qian et al. - 2019 - AUTOVC Zero-Shot Voice Style Transfer with Only Autoencoder Loss.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-06

---

## Definition

Zero-shot voice conversion means converting to the voice of an unseen speaker by using only a few utterances from that speaker as reference (source: Qian et al. - 2019 - AUTOVC Zero-Shot Voice Style Transfer with Only Autoencoder Loss.pdf). The AutoVC paper identifies zero-shot voice conversion as under-explored before its work and presents AutoVC as one of the first systems to perform it with decent performance (source: Qian et al. - 2019 - AUTOVC Zero-Shot Voice Style Transfer with Only Autoencoder Loss.pdf).

## AutoVC approach

[[autovc]] uses a pretrained speaker encoder instead of one-hot speaker IDs for zero-shot conversion (source: Qian et al. - 2019 - AUTOVC Zero-Shot Voice Style Transfer with Only Autoencoder Loss.pdf). In the paper's setup, target speaker embeddings are generated by averaging embeddings from 10 two-second utterances of the same speaker (source: Qian et al. - 2019 - AUTOVC Zero-Shot Voice Style Transfer with Only Autoencoder Loss.pdf).
